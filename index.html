01

The idea

You're surrounded by connected things — coffee machine, washer, fridge, lights, sensors. Each one carries an ARC identity and joins your network through an ARC router at home. Then you add apps and agents — weather, news, energy prices — and the whole thing becomes one ecosystem of devices, apps and agents that mostly runs locally. That local-first design is what makes it private and safe. AI-augmented life that lives in your house, not on someone's server.

A traffic agent keeps an eye on your commute. One morning it tells your home: "heavy traffic ahead — waking you 30 min early. Blinds opening, coffee brewing."

no vendor cloud · no hand-wired automation · everything runs in your home · you stay in control of what each agent can touch

02

Your home as a local network

To take part, every device and agent joins your home network through the ARC hub, which gives each one its own private address under taulant.arc and helps them discover one another. From there they talk directly, peer to peer — the hub introduces and relays, but never owns the conversation. Agents that need the internet reach in only through permissions you grant and can revoke.

03

Why ARC wins

ARC builds identity, discovery, trust and encrypted messaging into the network itself. Every device and every agent is a private, addressable participant you own — not an account on someone else's cloud.

Private by default

Your data and your routines live in your home, not on a vendor's servers. Nothing about your life is sold, mined, or leaked to the cloud.

Reliable when it matters

If one agent fails, the rest keep running — a misbehaving app never takes down the kitchen. The home is built to stay up.

Works without internet

The home runs locally. The internet can go down and your house keeps working — lights, coffee, locks and routines never miss a beat.

You're always in control

You decide exactly what each agent can touch — a traffic agent may start the coffee but never open the front door. Revoke access in one tap and it's gone.

05

The approach: white-label, native ARC

Rather than depend on brands exposing clean APIs, ARC ships in the hardware itself. A reference module — an ARC-Inside SoM plus SDK — drops into white-label devices so every appliance speaks ARC natively, offline, by design. One module, three phases, one motion.

Phase 1 · now

You drop the module into white-label devices and hand-build fully-native ARC environments — the flagship that shows what ARC is for.

Phase 2 · next

Hand the same module and spec to an ODM, who manufactures ARC-native units at volume. You stop soldering, start shipping.

Phase 3 · later

Brands integrate the module or license the firmware — they join your network instead of you begging for their API.

The open question this hangs on: is the endgame the module (a protocol company that stays light — the network is the asset) or the environments (a vertically-integrated AI-home brand you buy the whole house from)? First hire, first raise, and first 50 customers diverge completely from there.
